# AURA ARCHIVE POLICY

---

# PURPOSE

Define official archival and deprecation policy for AURA.

Goal:
- reduce architecture entropy
- isolate duplicate systems
- preserve experimental research
- protect production cognition
- support future recovery

---

# ARCHIVE PRINCIPLES

---

## PRINCIPLE 1
Never delete complex systems blindly.

---

## PRINCIPLE 2
Production cognition must remain isolated from experimental architecture.

---

## PRINCIPLE 3
Duplicate systems should be quarantined before deletion.

---

## PRINCIPLE 4
Research systems may retain future value.

---

## PRINCIPLE 5
Architecture stability is prioritized over rapid cleanup.

---

# OFFICIAL ARCHIVE TYPES

---

## LEGACY

Meaning:
Previously active systems replaced by canonical implementations.

Examples:
- duplicate memory systems
- old reasoning systems
- obsolete orchestration

---

## EXPERIMENTS

Meaning:
Research-oriented systems with future potential.

Examples:
- AGI prototypes
- autonomous cognition experiments
- world modeling research

---

## PARTIAL SYSTEMS

Meaning:
Incomplete but potentially valuable systems.

Examples:
- unfinished agents
- incomplete telemetry
- partial simulations

---

## DEPRECATED

Meaning:
Systems permanently retired.

These systems should no longer:
- receive updates
- receive integrations
- participate in production cognition

---

## FUTURE REVIEW

Meaning:
Systems pending future architectural review.

---

# ARCHIVE RULES

---

## RULE 1
Never archive production cognition accidentally.

---

## RULE 2
Canonical production cognition lives in:

```text
app/core
```

---

## RULE 3
Research systems remain isolated from production systems.

---

## RULE 4
Archived systems retain restoration capability.

---

## RULE 5
Imports must be reviewed before migration.

---

## RULE 6
No direct production dependency on archived systems.

---
